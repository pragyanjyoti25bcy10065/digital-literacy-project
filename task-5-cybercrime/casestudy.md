## Phishing Attack Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers impersonate trusted organizations to steal sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or OTPs.

A common example involves a student receiving an email or SMS that appears to be from a bank or payment service. The message usually creates urgency, such as claiming that the account will be blocked unless immediate action is taken. It contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website that looks identical to the original one.

When the user enters their login credentials or OTP, the attacker gains access to their account. This can result in financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized transactions.

Students and young users are often targeted because they frequently use digital platforms and may not verify links carefully. Phishing attacks are dangerous because they exploit human trust rather than technical vulnerabilities.
